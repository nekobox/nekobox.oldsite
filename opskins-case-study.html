    • OPSKINS


    Challenge

    The client, OPSKINS is looking to bring the ability to bring purchased items into the real world using AR technology. Implement a new 3D model marketplace for use in adult education with AR/VR technology for practical classroom use.

    Conditions
    • 2 weeks at General Assembly.
    Tools
    • SketchApp, InvisionApp, Krita, Material Palette, Google Drive, whiteboard, markers, Post-It notes
    My Roles

    C&C analysis, SWOT analysis, personas, user flow, sketching, high-fidelity InvisionApp prototype.

    Solution

    A clickable prototype that shares the familiar utility of PowerPoint while easing users into the integration of AR elements into their lesson planning.


    Design Process

    Step 1: What is OPSKINS?

    We begin with a SWOT analysis of OPSKINS to better understand their existing service and products as well as to identify areas of opportunity that we can leverage in this build.

    A C&C Analysis was completed to identify local/regional competitors, their practices (those that work as well as those that don't), highlight commonly shared features, and where market gaps exist that 'Lette can leverage in their own digital presence.

    I create a Task Analysis of a user purchasing a dozen macarons to identify where their pain points are. This informs the User Journey Map that I create to illustrate and put into scope 'Lette's shortcomings and areas of opportunity.

    Understanding the user's pain points and integrating findings from the above-referenced research techniques, I create a Problem Statement: 'Lette is aiming to improve their website's e-commerce presence order to improve their users' macaron-ordering experience. However, 'Lette Macaronâ€™s current dashboard experience is not intuitive and it places too many barriers between the user and their end goal.

    Step 2: Research

    I make a deep dive into Information Architecture and begin to conduct both open and closed Card Sorts to determine intuitive categorizations for the website's primary navigation.

    A Persona was created to humanize and focus on the scope of my design process- WHO am I designing for? I decide on "Kelsey": an event planner who needs to be able to place an immediate order from her desk.

    Step 3: Ideation- What does any of it mean???

    With the problem and user in mind, we are able to come up with a Solution Statement to have a goal to work towards: To address this problem is to redesign the primary navigation to clearly define what products are available and their individual information. This interface will help the user identify what they're trying to purchase, while expediting their checkout experience.


    To maintain scope, I identify a Success Metric:

    • User completes an order starting from 'Lette Macaron's landing page and ending at the order confirmation.

    I create a User Flow based on how Kelsey may use an online checkout.

    In sorting around common features, I realize there are different interview stages I need to design for. I create a Feature Prioritization graph to mark the importance and visibility of each feature. I narrowed down the user's needs to 3-4 main features.

    Step 4: Usability Testing

    I begin testing with a Paper Prototype in order to iterate and re-iterate.

    Based on my findings, I create a Wireframe that I use to inform the higher fidelity prototype.

    Step 5: The Solution

    I create a clickable Prototype in SketchApp and InvisionApp that reflects my findings. I test and iterate on the higher fidelity version before presenting my current solution to my cohort at General Assembly.
