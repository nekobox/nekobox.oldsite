    • OPSKINS


    Challenge

    The client, OPSKINS is looking to bring the ability to bring purchased items into the real world using AR technology- implement a new 3D model marketplace for use in adult education with AR/VR technology for practical classroom use.

    Conditions
    • 2 weeks at General Assembly.
    Tools
    • SketchApp, InvisionApp, Krita, Material Palette, Google Drive, whiteboard, markers, Post-It notes
    My Roles

    C&C analysis, SWOT analysis, personas, user flow, sketching, high-fidelity InvisionApp prototype.

    Solution

    A clickable prototype that shares the familiar utility of PowerPoint while easing users into the integration of AR elements into their lesson planning.


    Design Process

    Step 1: What is OPSKINS?

    OPSKINS is a 3rd-party online marketplace that sells digital assets in the form of aesthetic "skins" in various games. This challenge is a pivot from their current target demographic in gaming and a point of focus for our team as we begin our design.

    We determined that a SWOT analysis of OPSKINS is the next appropriate step in order to understand their existing service as well as to identify areas of opportunity that we can leverage in our design.

    Step 2: Research

    Without knowing enough about what is possible with educational AR, our team decided to conduct a C&C Analysis to identify educational AR businesses and/or services to highlight commonly shared features as well as what can realistically be leveraged in our application with commercially-available technology.

    Equipped with a better understanding of the possibilities and constraints of AR technology and that educational AR applications are out there, we narrowed our scope through a Feature Prioritization.

    Understanding the user's pain points and integrating findings from the above-referenced research techniques, I create a Problem Statement: 'Lette is aiming to improve their website's e-commerce presence order to improve their users' macaron-ordering experience. However, 'Lette Macaronâ€™s current dashboard experience is not intuitive and it places too many barriers between the user and their end goal.

    Our team makes a deep dive into User Research where we surveyed educators, students, and 3D-model artists.

    Informed by our surveys and interviews, we create a Persona based on a collection of recurring traits in our user research. This allows us to maintain the scope of our project as well as ensure that this iteration will truly resolve our user's problems. "Jonathan": an adjunct professor who needs struggles with balancing student engagement and classroom resources with his own time and resources.

    Step 3: Ideation- Making Sense of the Data

    With the problem and user in mind, we are able to come up with a Solution Statement to have a goal to work towards: To address this challenge is to pivot away from OPSKINS' gaming background and to create a new platform with a resource to drive the need for a marketplace of AR assets.


    To maintain our scope, we identify a Success Metric:

    • User is able to create, utilize AR assets, and publish a lesson deck using our interface.

    We create a User Flow based on how Jonathan might use our lesson deck builder.

    Step 4: Usability Testing

    We begin testing with a Paper Prototype in order to iterate and re-iterate.

    Based on our findings, we create a Wireframe that we use to inform the higher fidelity prototype.

    Step 5: The Solution

    We create a clickable Prototype in SketchApp and InvisionApp that reflects our findings. We test and iterate on the higher fidelity version before presenting my current solution to our cohort at General Assembly.
