JOBS FOR HOPE


Challenge

The 'Jobs' section of the Los Angeles County Homeless Initiative’s website is difficult to navigate and provides inconsistent job listings between organizations. Job seekers are thus deterred from applying and providing their skills in high-need jobs assisting this vulnerable community.

Conditions
  • 2 week design sprint which includes a pitch deck.
Tools
  • SketchApp, InvisionApp, Illustrator, Google Drive, Google Slides, GitHub, Visual Studio Code, sketch pad.
My Responsibilities

Heuristic analysis, C&C analysis, task analysis, site map, mid-fidelity prototype, pitch deck.

Solution

A redesigned 'Jobs' section that streamlines the usability and search experience of skilled individuals seeking jobs that directly assist the homeless community in Los Angeles.

View Prototype

Step 1: Research

CURRENT WEBSITE EVALUATION

Using Jacob Nielsen’s 10 heuristics of usability I evaluated the LA County Homeless Initiative’s current job portal and compiled a list of opportunities to address. In the chart below are specifics of some of the key issues, which heuristic was violated, and a measure of how severe this problem would be for the user.

A C&C Analysis was completed to identify the differences between the 'Jobs for Hope' features and layout compared to 3 other major job search databases.

TASK ANALYSIS

I create a Task Analysis of a user trying to find a job as a 'Manager' on the existing portal. This informs the User Journey Map that I create to illustrate and put into scope the current site's shortcomings and areas of opportunity.

Understanding the user's pain points and integrating findings from the above-referenced research techniques, I create a Problem Statement: 'Lette is aiming to improve their website's e-commerce presence order to improve their users' macaron-ordering experience. However, 'Lette Macaron’s current dashboard experience is not intuitive and it places too many barriers between the user and their end goal.

Step 3: Making Sense of My Findings

With the problem and user in mind, I was able to come up with a Solution Statement to work towards: To address this problem is to redesign the primary navigation to clearly define what products are available and their individual information. This interface will help the user identify what they're trying to purchase, while expediting their checkout experience.


To maintain scope, I identify a Success Metric:

  • User completes an order starting from 'Lette Macaron's landing page and ending at the order confirmation.

Step 4: User Testing

I begin testing with a Paper Prototype in order to iterate and re-iterate.

Based on my findings, I create a Wireframe that I use to inform the higher fidelity prototype.

Step 5: The Solution

I create a clickable Prototype in SketchApp and InvisionApp and incorporate it into the pitch deck to be presented to one of the directors of the Los Angeles County Homeless Initiative.
